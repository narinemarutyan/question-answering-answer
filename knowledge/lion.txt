Lion Information

Lions are large carnivorous mammals known as the “king of the jungle.”
They are part of the big cat family and are recognized for their
muscular build, powerful roar, and social group behavior.

Key facts: - Lifespan: 10–14 years in the wild, up to 20 years in
captivity - Diet: Carnivorous, primarily hunting large herbivores like
zebras and wildebeests - Behavior: Live in groups called prides,
consisting of related females, their offspring, and a few males -
Health: Threats include habitat loss, human conflict, and disease

Lions are iconic animals symbolizing strength and bravery. They require
vast territories and balanced ecosystems to survive in the wild.
